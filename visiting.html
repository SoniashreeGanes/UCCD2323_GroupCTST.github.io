Visiting Etiquette

Open-Door Policy

Malaysians are known for their open-door policy, which means that friends and family are always welcome to visit each other's homes, often unannounced. It's common for people to drop by for a chat, a cup of tea, or a meal without prior arrangements.

Remove Shoes Before Entering Into A House

It is customary to remove your shoes before entering a Malaysian home. Many homes have a designated area for shoes, and it's a sign of respect to follow this practice.

No Strict Punctuality

Malaysians typically don't place a strong emphasis on punctuality when visiting someone's home. While hosts may suggest a time, it's generally understood that guests may arrive late, and this is perfectly acceptable.

Bringing Gifts

When visiting someone's home, it's customary to bring a small gift as a token of appreciation for the hospitality. Common gifts include fruits, sweets, or crafts. These gifts are known as 'buah tangan,' which translates to "fruit of the hands."

Refreshments

Hosts will often prepare refreshments such as tea, coffee, and snacks for their guests. It's polite to wait for the host to offer these refreshments before helping yourself.

Bringing Food Is Optional

When invited for a meal, guests are not expected to bring dishes or food items unless it's a potluck-style gathering. The host takes pride in preparing a variety of dishes for their guests.
